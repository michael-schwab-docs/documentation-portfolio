# AI-Assisted SOP Development

## Workflow and Governance Framework

  

---

  

## Purpose

  

This document outlines a structured approach for using AI tools to support the development of standard operating procedures (SOPs). The goal is to improve drafting efficiency while maintaining accuracy, consistency, and appropriate human oversight.

  

This framework incorporates observations from an initial application to better reflect how AI-generated outputs behave in structured documentation workflows.

  

---

  

## Scope

  

This framework applies to the use of AI tools in drafting, refining, and standardizing SOP documentation.

  

It is intended for use by professionals responsible for:

  

- Developing process documentation

- Maintaining documentation standards

- Supporting operational or analytical workflows

  

This framework does not replace domain expertise, governance policies, or formal approval processes.

  

---

  

## When to Use AI

  

AI tools may be used to support:

  

- Drafting initial procedure structure from defined inputs

- Reorganizing or standardizing existing documentation

- Clarifying or rewording complex or ambiguous steps

- Converting informal notes into structured formats

  

AI should not be used as a substitute for subject matter expertise or final validation.

  

---

  

## Workflow

  

AI is used within a defined, human-led process.

  

### 1. Define Outcome

  

Identify:

  

- The intended result of the procedure

- Required inputs

- Success criteria

  

### 2. Draft with AI

  

Generate an initial draft using a structured prompt. Inputs should include:

  

- Known process steps

- Constraints

- Required output format

  

### 3. Validate (Human Review)

  

Review for:

  

- Accuracy

- Completeness

- Alignment with actual practice

  

### 4. Refine with AI

  

Use targeted prompts to:

  

- Improve clarity

- Standardize structure

- Reduce ambiguity

  

Do not introduce unverified content.

  

### 5. Finalize and Approve

  

- Apply formatting standards

- Complete final review

- Document version as required

  

---

  

## Prompt Framework

  

AI outputs are highly dependent on input quality and constraint clarity. A structured prompt framework improves consistency and reduces ambiguity.

  

### Template

  

**Role:** You are a process documentation specialist in \[domain\].

  

**Objective:** Draft a standard operating procedure for \[process\].

  

**Context:**

  

- The procedure supports \[business function or outcome\]

- The intended users are \[role or audience\]

  

**Constraints:**

  

- Do not assume missing steps

- Flag any uncertainty or incomplete inputs

- Maintain alignment with provided information only

- Where required inputs are not provided, flag the gap rather than relying on conditional statements

- Use consistent terminology throughout the document

- Limit the procedure strictly to the defined scope

  

**Output Format:**

  

- Purpose

- Scope

- Definitions (if applicable)

- Procedure (step-by-step)

- Exceptions or notes

  

Prompts should be adapted based on process complexity and available inputs.

  

---

  

## Human Review Requirements

  

AI-generated content must be reviewed prior to use or distribution. At minimum:

  

- Confirm all steps are accurate and reflect actual processes

- Identify gaps, omissions, or inconsistencies

- Validate terminology and domain-specific language

- Ensure alignment with internal standards

  

The level of review should be proportionate to the risk and impact of the procedure. Higher-risk or compliance-sensitive processes may require more detailed validation and subject matter expert review.

  

Where AI is used to revise existing procedures, outputs should be compared against prior versions to confirm that changes are intentional and appropriately validated.

  

AI output should be treated as a draft, not a final deliverable.

  

---

  

## Observed Output Patterns

  

An initial application of this framework across multiple AI tools produced consistent patterns:

  

- **Terminology variation:** Definitions varied significantly across tools, even with similar prompts.

- **Scope expansion:** Some tools introduced additional steps beyond the defined objective.

- **Inconsistent risk signaling:** Tools differed in how uncertainty was communicated.

- **Conditional overuse:** Outputs relied heavily on conditional language when inputs were undefined.

  

These observations informed refinements to prompt constraints to improve scope control, terminology consistency, and output usability.

  

---

  

## Risks and Considerations

  

The use of AI in documentation introduces specific risks:

  

- **Hallucination:** AI may generate plausible but incorrect steps

- **Omission:** Critical steps or edge cases may be excluded

- **False completeness:** Outputs may appear complete despite missing inputs or unresolved decisions

- **Data sensitivity:** Inputs should not include confidential or proprietary information unless permitted under applicable data handling policies

  

---

  

## Key Principles

  

- AI is assistive, not authoritative

- Output quality is driven by input definition

- Undefined inputs produce conditional logic rather than resolved decisions

- Human validation is required for all final content

- Consistency and repeatability take priority over speed

  

---

  

## Summary

  

AI can support SOP development when used within a structured, controlled workflow. This approach emphasizes clarity, validation, and governance to ensure documentation remains accurate and fit for purpose.
